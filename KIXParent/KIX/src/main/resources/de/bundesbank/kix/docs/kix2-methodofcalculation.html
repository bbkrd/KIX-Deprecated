KIX 2.0 - Method of calculation

Version:2015-06-24

Aggregation of chain indices with plus and minus

Enter: [Name=]KIX,i1,w1,+/-,i2,w2,...,refyr

Execution of the calculation loops:

  • Entries are read into the system and evaluated from left to right, ie starting with i1, w1.
    Each loop operation produces two interim values in the form of an aggregated index and a
  • weight (simple +/-) which can then be used for the next addition or subtraction.
  • As a final step the last calculated index is normalised to match with the reference year (refyr).

Note:If you use different starting years for the KIX formula this may cause the entry sequence to
influence the first year of the final result wherever more than two input indices exist.
This effect stems from the fact that interim results contain starting years that are sequentially dependent.

Aggregating two indices (i1, w1) +/- (i2, w2)

Firstly, a check is made for the following conditions:

  • weight series must not begin after corresponding index series
    (However, all weight values preceding the latest index starting time are ignored during calculation.)
  • during the first year index series must be defined from their first period onward

As a next step both index series are unchained.
These unchained series are then aggregated, ie a weighted total or difference is calculated on a value-by-value basis
To do this the previous year averages from the weight series are applied as weights.
The resulting series is then chain-linked and normalised.

Calculating growth contributions

Enter: [Name=]WBG,iContr,wContr,+/-,iTotal,wTotal,#ofLags

Firstly,a check is made for the following conditions:

  • weight series must not start later than corresponding index series
    (However, any weight values positioned before the latest index starting point are excluded from the calculation.)
  • for their first year index series must be defined from the first period onward
  • the iContr series must not start later than iTotal

As a next step, iTotal is normalised to the year when it started in order to enable subsequent comparison with an auxiliary series.
Following this we calculate both auxiliary series iRes and wRes.

  • iRes represents
    • for a positive entry "+": the difference between two indices iTotal "-" iContr
    • for a negative entry "-": the sum of two indices iTotal "+" iContr
    (In each case both indices are unchained then weighted using the previous year averages for wTotal and wContr
    in order to create the sum or difference. iRes is left unchained.)
  • wRes represents
    • for a positive entry "+": the simple difference between two time series wTotal - wContr
    • for a negative entry "-": the simple sum of two time series wTotal + wContr

As a next step, iContr is lagged with #ofLags periods.
The lagged series is unchained using the previous year averages from the unlagged series.

This series is then weighted together with iRes as follows:

  • for a positive entry "+": as weighted sum
  • for a negative entry "-": as weighted difference

To do this the previous year averages for wContr (unlagged!) and wRes are applied as weights.
The resulting aggregate is then chain-linked, though in this case using the previous year averages from the unchained iTotal.
As an interim result we get the time series iTotalLag. This series is normalised to match its starting year; it therefore can be compared with iTotal.
Now we determine the relative differences to the #OfLags-th preceding period of iTotal for both iTotal and iTotalLag.
The final growth contribution figure is produced by subtracting the difference for iTotalLag from the difference for iTotal.

Auxiliary functions

Normalising a time series to match a reference year

Normalising the average of the unadjusted data in the reference year to 100 yields the values
of the aggregate in the reference year as the weighted sum of the index values of the components, whereby the share of
the reference year volumes (valued at the average prices in the previous year) yields the weighting scheme.
However, as the volumes used as weights are not available directly, they are calculated as the product of the nominal
figures from the previous year and the change factor of the annual volumes.

Unchaining a series

Each value in the time series is divided by the respective previous year average then multiplied by 100.
This can be achieved only if both the values themselves and their previous year averages are defined.
This results in a series where each year refers to "previous year=100".
When calculating growth contributions steps may be applied where the previous year averages are not calculated
on the basis of the actual time series but using a second, chain-linked time series instead.

Chain-linking a series

Each value in the series is multiplied by the cumulated previous year averages then divided by 100.
This can be achieved only if both the value itself and all of the previous year averages(from the start of the series) are defined.
This results in a series where each year refers to "first year=100".
When calculating growth contributions steps may be applied where the previous year averages are not calculated
on the basis of the actual time series but using a second, unchained time series instead.

Previous year averages

By previous year average we mean the unweighted average of all values recorded in the preceding year.
It is calculated only if all of the values for that year are available.
For the first defined year of a time series the previous year average is equal to the annual average.

are thus determined. The result can be determined only if both values are already defined and the second value is greater than zero.

Additional Features

  • Naming Formulas: You can name an resulting time series by writing "Your Name of choice"="Formula" into the text area.
  • Autocompleted weights: If you don't choose a specific weight series for your aggregation, the weight series with the same number will be taken.
